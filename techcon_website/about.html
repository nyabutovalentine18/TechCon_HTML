Our History

From a small meetup to a global technology movement

TechCon began in 2012 when a group of passionate developers and entrepreneurs recognized the need for a platform where emerging technologies could be shared and discussed openly. What started as a 300-person gathering in Austin, Texas has blossomed into an international phenomenon attracting thousands of participants annually.

Our growth mirrors the rapid evolution of the tech landscape. We've expanded from focusing primarily on web development to embracing artificial intelligence, blockchain, quantum computing, and sustainable technology solutions. Each year, we've adapted to include the most relevant and transformative technologies shaping our future.

TechCon 2012 - Our humble beginnings in Austin

TechCon 2017 - Expanding to Europe with our Berlin event

TechCon 2023 - Our largest event yet in Singapore

Throughout our evolution, we've maintained our core commitment to fostering genuine connections between innovators. Our community has launched countless collaborations, startups, and open-source projects that continue to push technological boundaries worldwide.

Our Mission

Driving meaningful innovation for global impact

At TechCon, we believe technology should serve humanity, not the other way around. Our mission is to create a space where ethical considerations guide technological advancement, and where diverse voices collaborate to solve the world's most pressing challenges.

Our guiding principles shape every aspect of TechCon:

  • Human-Centered Innovation: We prioritize technologies that enhance human potential and address real-world needs.
  • Inclusive Progress: We actively work to break down barriers in tech and create opportunities for underrepresented groups.
  • Sustainable Development: We champion technologies that promote environmental stewardship and circular economies.
  • Collaborative Discovery: We believe the most transformative ideas emerge when diverse perspectives intersect.

Past Speakers

Industry pioneers who have shaped TechCon

TechCon has been privileged to host some of the most influential minds in technology. Our speakers have included Nobel laureates, Turing Award winners, startup founders, and leaders from the world's most innovative companies.

Dr. Elena Rodriguez

AI Research Pioneer, NeuroTech Labs

Dr. Rodriguez's groundbreaking work in neural networks has revolutionized how machines process complex information. Her 2021 TechCon keynote on ethical AI frameworks set industry standards for responsible development.

Sarah Chen

Founding Partner, Global Tech Ventures

A serial entrepreneur turned venture capitalist, Chen has funded over 50 tech startups focused on solving global challenges. Her 2019 talk on "Impact Investing in Technology" inspired a generation of socially-conscious founders.

Marcus Johnson

Chief Scientist, QuantumLeap Inc.

Johnson's research has pushed the boundaries of quantum computing applications. His 2022 demonstration of quantum encryption at TechCon showcased technology that could redefine digital security worldwide.

Dr. Aisha Patel

CEO, BioSynth Technologies

Dr. Patel's work at the intersection of biology and technology has led to breakthroughs in personalized medicine. Her 2020 presentation on AI-driven drug discovery highlighted how technology can accelerate medical innovation.

James Wilson

CTO, OpenFuture Foundation

A leading voice in the open source movement, Wilson has championed collaborative development models. His 2018 TechCon talk on "Democratizing Technology" influenced corporate open source policies globally.

Dr. Li Wei

Director, GreenTech Institute

Dr. Li's research focuses on technology solutions for climate change. Her 2023 presentation on carbon capture innovations demonstrated how tech can play a crucial role in environmental sustainability.

These visionary thinkers represent just a fraction of the incredible talent that has graced the TechCon stage. Each has contributed unique perspectives that have shaped not only our conference but the direction of technology itself.
